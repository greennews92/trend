Question 1: What is Down syndrome?


Answer: Down syndrome is a genetic condition that occurs when a person has an extra copy of chromosome 21. This extra copy of the chromosome causes developmental delays and intellectual disabilities, as well as some physical characteristics such as a flattened facial profile and an enlarged tongue.


Question 2: How does Down syndrome affect individuals?


Answer: Down syndrome affects individuals in different ways. Some individuals with Down syndrome may have mild intellectual disabilities, while others may have more severe disabilities. Individuals with Down syndrome may also have physical health problems, such as heart defects and hearing loss.
