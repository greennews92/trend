Type of Twins

Fraternal twins, also known as dizygotic twins, are a type of twins that result from the fertilization of two separate eggs by two different sperm. This is in contrast to identical twins, who develop from a single fertilized egg that splits into two embryos.

  • Genetic Uniqueness: Fraternal twins share approximately 50% of their genes, similar to siblings born at different times. This means that they are genetically unique individuals, with their own distinct physical and personality traits.
  • Physical Differences: Fraternal twins can be of the same sex or opposite sexes. They may have different physical characteristics, such as hair color, eye color, and facial features.
  • Developmental Milestones: Fraternal twins may reach developmental milestones at different rates. This is because they are not genetically identical and may have slightly different growth patterns.
  • Bonding and Relationships: Fraternal twins often share a close bond, but their relationship may be different from that of identical twins. They may have their own individual interests and friendships.

In conclusion, fraternal twins are unique individuals with their own genetic makeup and physical characteristics. The Clooney twins, Ella and Alexander, are fraternal twins who share a close bond and are growing and developing into their own distinct personalities.
