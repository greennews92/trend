Who is Dorothy Barton?

Dorothy Barton is an American author best known for her historical novels set in the American West. Her books have been praised for their vivid descriptions of the landscape and their strong female characters.

Barton was born in 1951 in Texas. She grew up on a ranch, and her experiences there inspired her to write about the American West. Her first novel, The Widow's Walk, was published in 1989. It tells the story of a young woman who travels to the Wyoming Territory in the 1870s to find her husband. The novel was a critical and commercial success, and it established Barton as a major voice in Western literature.

Barton's other novels include The House on Bear Creek (1993), The Burning Sky (1999), and The Song of the Lark (2005). Her work has been translated into more than 20 languages, and she has won numerous awards, including the Western Writers of America Spur Award and the Mountains and Plains Booksellers Association Award.

Dorothy Barton is a gifted storyteller who brings the American West to life in her novels. Her books are full of adventure, romance, and history, and they are sure to appeal to readers of all ages.
