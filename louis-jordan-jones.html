Who was Louis Jordan Jones?

Louis Jordan Jones was an African-American inventor who patented the ice cream scoop in 1897. His invention revolutionized the way ice cream was served and made it more accessible to the general public.

Before Jones' invention, ice cream was typically served in large blocks or bricks, which were difficult to scoop and portion. Jones' ice cream scoop featured a semi-circular bowl with a spring-loaded lever that ejected the ice cream in perfectly round scoops. This made it much easier to serve ice cream and helped to popularize the dessert.

Jones' ice cream scoop was a major commercial success, and he eventually sold the rights to his patent to a company that manufactured ice cream scoops. His invention continues to be used today, and it is estimated that over 90% of the ice cream scoops in use today are based on Jones' original design.
